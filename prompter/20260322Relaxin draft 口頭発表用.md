I would have presentation about “Relaxin Family Peptide 1 as a Pathogenic Factor for Femoroacetabular Impingement: Expression in the Murine Hip Joint”

Relaxin is a peptide hormone that binds to relaxin family peptide receptor 1 (RXFP1) and induces relaxation of the pubic ligament through the production of matrix metalloproteinases and collagen degradation.

This hormone’s ligament-relaxing effect may affect the hip joint, **particularly in patients with** femoroacetabular impingement (FAI).

The objectives of this study are: To examine sex differences and pregnancy-related variations of RXFP1 in the hip joint, and to investigate the relationship between chondrocyte differentiation and RXFP1 expression.

Methods
in vivo study
Male and female 8-week-old mice and mice at 18 days of gestation were used. RXFP1 expression was examined by RT-PCR, and histological evaluations were performed using HE and MT staining. Immunohistochemistry was performed using antibodies against RXFP1, vimentin, and SOX9.
Blood samples were collected, and serum relaxin concentrations were measured.

in vitro
In experiments using cultured cells, ATDC5 cells were induced to differentiate using ITS as a chondrogenic inducer. Nodule formation was evaluated by Alcian blue staining, and the expression of RXFP1 and SOX9 was examined by Western blotting.

Results

First, please look at the **top-left panel** showing the histological evaluation. As you can see, there were no obvious morphological differences between males and females.

Moving to the **top-right panel**, immunohistochemical analysis detected RXFP1 expression in both the articular cartilage and the joint capsule. These positive signals are indicated by the **black arrowheads**.

To further characterize these cells, we performed double immunostaining, shown in the **bottom-left panel**. Here, we found that RXFP1 colocalized with vimentin, a mesenchymal cell marker, as well as the chondrocyte marker SOX9. The **yellow arrowheads** clearly indicate this colocalization.

Consistent with these histological findings, the **Western blot results on the bottom-right** demonstrate that the expression levels of both RXFP1 and SOX9 increased as the culture period progressed.

Conclusion

In this study, we demonstrated
RXFP1 expression is localized in the articular cartilage and joint capsule.
RXFP1-expressing cells include fibroblasts and chondrocytes.
RXFP1 expression is upregulated according to cell differentiation.
